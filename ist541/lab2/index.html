How to Create a Graphic Organizer

By Kristen Jordan





Step 1: Identify the Topic or Central Idea

Begin by choosing the topic you’re organizing information about. This could be a lesson, a concept, or a research topic.

warning Write this in the center of your graphic organizer.



Step 2: Choose the Type of Graphic Organizer

Decide which type of graphic organizer suits your needs. Options include a mind map (for brainstorming), Venn diagram (for comparing and contrasting), or flowchart (for showing processes).



Step 3: Brainstorm and Add Key Ideas

Write down the main ideas or categories related to your topic. Place these around your central topic or in the appropriate sections of your chosen organizer.



Step 4: Organize Information Logically

Organize your ideas by grouping similar concepts together. Use lines, arrows, or sections to visually represent relationships between ideas.



Step 5: Include Specific Details or Examples

Add supporting details or examples under each key idea. For example, under “Producers,” you could add specific examples like “Trees” or “Grass.”



Step 6: Review and Refine Your Graphic Organizer

Review your graphic organizer to ensure it clearly represents the topic and its relationships. Refine any sections by adding, removing, or reorganizing information if needed.



Step 7: Add Colors, Shapes, or Symbols for Clarity

Use colors, shapes, or symbols to make your graphic organizer easier to read and more engaging. For example, use different colors for each section or arrows to show cause and effect.



Step 8: Present and Use Your Graphic Organizer

Use your graphic organizer to explain the topic to others or to study from. You can present it to the class, use it for a project, or review it for exams.
