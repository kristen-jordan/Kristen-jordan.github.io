How to Use This Course

This course is organized into a short overview and six modules. Each module is built in iSpring and then posted on GitHub as a web-based lesson you can open in your browser.

1. Recommended path

  • Begin at the Course Overview for a quick orientation.
  • Continue through Modules 1–6 in order to see the full progression.
  • Return to any module later when revising or designing your own lessons.

2. Opening a module

  • Go to the Modules page.
  • Select the card or link for the module you want to open (for example, Module 2 – The Three Principles of UDL).
  • The iSpring lesson will load as a web page. Depending on your browser settings, it may open in the same tab or a new tab.
If a module does not appear after you click the link, see Navigation Help for troubleshooting steps.

3. Using iSpring player controls

Each module is delivered through the iSpring player. Controls may vary slightly by module, but commonly include:

  • Next / Previous buttons to move through slides or sections.
  • Play / Pause controls for audio narration or video segments.
  • A visual progress indicator (for example, a timeline or slide list).

Use the on-screen controls to move at your own pace. You can pause at any point to take notes or reflect before continuing.

4. Working with activities and questions

Some modules include interactive checks, reflection prompts, or scenario-based questions. These are intended as practice and reflection opportunities, not high-stakes assessments.

  • Read the prompt carefully before responding.
  • Use these as a space to experiment with ideas and connect UDL + AI to your own context.
  • When possible, jot down examples from your teaching that come to mind.

5. Taking notes as you go

You may find it helpful to keep a simple note document or template open as you move through the modules. You can:

  • Capture key UDL principles or checkpoints that feel relevant for your learners.
  • List lesson ideas you want to revisit or redesign.
  • Record any AI tools you want to explore further.

Quick Reference

Use this checklist as a simple guide each time you work in the course.

Before you start
  • Have a stable internet connection.
  • Use a modern browser (for example, Chrome, Edge, or Firefox).
  • Turn on your audio if the module includes narration.
When you open a module
  • Locate the module controls (Next/Previous, Play/Pause).
  • Scan the estimated time listed on the Modules page.
  • Decide whether you’ll complete the whole module now or part of it.
As you work
  • Pause when you want more time to think or take notes.
  • Capture at least one idea you might try in your own context.
  • Note any questions you’d like to explore further.
If something isn’t working
  • Refresh the page once.
  • Check your internet connection and volume settings.
  • Visit Navigation Help for more detailed troubleshooting.

When in doubt, start at the Modules page and follow the recommended order. You can always return to modules later to review key ideas or examples.
