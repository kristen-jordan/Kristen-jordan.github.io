Teaching for Every Learner
Universal Design for Learning & the Power of AI

A professional learning course for K–12 educators integrating UDL and AI to support flexible, inclusive instruction.

Self-paced · 6 Modules

Design for every learner, with UDL and AI.

Learn how to apply Universal Design for Learning and use AI as a supportive tool to reduce barriers, increase engagement, and honor learner variability.

  • Ground your practice in the three UDL principles.
  • Redesign lessons using UDL-aligned templates and examples.
  • Explore AI tools that extend accessibility and learner choice.

Overview

Course Overview

Orientation to structure, objectives, and navigation.

Get a quick tour of the course, who it’s for, and what you’ll take away. This overview also introduces how UDL and AI are woven throughout each module.

Introduction to UDL

Why universal design matters for every learner.

Explore the core ideas behind Universal Design for Learning, learner variability, and the shift from “fixing” students to redesigning learning environments.

Module 2

The Three Principles of UDL

Engagement, representation, and action & expression.

Take a closer look at the UDL principles and guidelines, and connect them to real classroom examples you can recognize in your own practice.

Module 3

Applying UDL in Lesson Design

From theory to lesson-plan decisions.

Use a simple UDL lesson-planning structure to rework or enhance one of your existing lessons, with attention to goals, methods, materials, and assessment.

Module 4

AI as a Tool for UDL

Leveraging AI to support flexible design.

Explore practical ways AI can help generate options, reduce barriers, and personalize supports—while staying grounded in UDL and ethical use.

Go to Module 4↗ Approx. 10–15 min
Module 5

Designing for Variability

Scenario-based practice and reflection.

Work through short scenarios to practice spotting potential barriers, brainstorm UDL-aligned options, and refine your design choices with AI support.

Module 6

Wrap-Up & Assessment

Consolidate learning and plan next steps.
