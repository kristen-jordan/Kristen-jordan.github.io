Self-paced · 6 modules + overview

Design flexible, inclusive learning with UDL and AI.

This course introduces Universal Design for Learning (UDL) and shows how AI tools can extend access, engagement, and choice—while keeping ethics and equity at the center.

  • Learn the core concepts and principles of UDL.
  • Apply UDL to real lessons and scenarios.
  • Explore how AI can support UDL-aligned design decisions.
View Course Modules Start with Course Overview
Approximate total time: about 60–75 minutes, completed in short modules at your own pace.

Who this course is for:

Designed primarily for K–12 educators, instructional designers, and support staff who want to plan for learner variability from the start.

  • You are curious about UDL and want clear, classroom-ready examples.
  • You’d like to redesign at least one lesson or unit with UDL in mind.
  • You want practical ways to use AI tools to support inclusion and access.

Course Modules Overview

A quick look at what each module covers. Visit the Modules page for full descriptions.

  • Course Overview — Orientation to the course structure, navigation, and key themes.
  • Module 1: Introduction to UDL — Foundational concepts and learner variability.
  • Module 2: The Three Principles of UDL — Engagement, Representation, and Action & Expression.
  • Module 3: Applying UDL in Lesson Design — Planning goals, methods, materials, and assessments.
  • Module 4: AI as a Tool for UDL — Ways AI can support UDL-aligned instructional design.
  • Module 5: Designing for Variability — Scenario-based practice applying UDL + AI together.
  • Module 6: Wrap-Up & Assessment — Reflection, key takeaways, and next steps.

To explore each module in detail, visit the Modules page.

 

What you’ll need

A simple setup is enough—modules are designed to be accessible and easy to navigate.

Technology

  • Modern web browser (for example, Chrome, Edge, or Firefox).
  • Reliable internet connection.
  • Speakers or headphones for audio narration and video.

Time & Pacing

  • Modules are short and focused—about 10–11 minutes each.
  • You can complete them in sequence or spread them across several sessions.
  • You may pause, rewind, and revisit modules at any time.

Optional Materials

  • A lesson or unit you’d like to redesign using UDL.
  • A simple note document to capture ideas and action steps.
  • Access to any AI tools allowed in your context for experimentation.
